# CS 250 Portfolio – Sprint Review & Retrospective

## Overview
This repository contains my Sprint Review and Retrospective from the SNHU Travel booking software project. The project demonstrates the application of Agile Scrum practices, including iterative development, collaboration, and adaptability throughout the software development life cycle.

## Project Summary
The Scrum Agile framework played a significant role in the development of the SNHU Travel booking system. Throughout the project, I took on multiple roles including Scrum Master, Product Owner, tester, and developer. This experience provided insight into how each role contributes to the success of a software development team and highlighted the importance of collaboration, communication, and adaptability.

## Roles and Contributions

### Scrum Master
As Scrum Master, I guided the team in following Scrum practices and facilitated key events such as Sprint Planning, Daily Scrums, Sprint Reviews, and Retrospectives. These activities ensured the team remained organized, focused, and aligned with project goals.

### Product Owner
In this role, I focused on understanding user needs and translating them into actionable user stories. Stakeholder feedback helped prioritize important features such as flexible date searches and transparent pricing.

### Tester
As a tester, I developed test cases based on user stories and acceptance criteria. This emphasized the importance of clear requirements, as missing details made it more difficult to create effective tests.

### Developer
In the developer role, I implemented features while adapting to changing requirements and collaborating with other roles. This required flexibility and strong communication.

## Agile Process and Effectiveness
The Agile approach allowed the team to break down large features into smaller, manageable tasks completed over multiple sprints. For example, the booking system was developed incrementally by focusing on individual components such as search functionality, passenger input, and pricing.

Sprint Planning and backlog refinement ensured tasks were clearly defined, while acceptance criteria provided clear expectations for development and testing. This structure supported consistent progress and delivery of user value.

## Adapting to Change
Agile proved especially effective in handling changing requirements. Updates to features, such as flexible date searches, were managed by adjusting the product backlog and incorporating changes into future sprints. This iterative approach allowed the team to remain productive without major disruptions.

## Communication and Collaboration
Communication was essential throughout the project. Daily Scrums helped the team stay aligned and quickly address obstacles. Collaboration between roles ensured that requirements were clearly understood and implemented correctly.

Clear communication reduced misunderstandings, improved efficiency, and strengthened teamwork.

## Tools and Scrum Events
Tools such as Jira or Azure Boards acted as information radiators, improving visibility into tasks and progress. Scrum events—including Sprint Planning, Daily Scrums, Sprint Reviews, and Retrospectives—provided structure and supported continuous improvement.

## Advantages and Challenges of Agile

### Advantages
- Flexibility in responding to changing requirements  
- Continuous feedback from stakeholders  
- Incremental development and testing  
- Improved product quality  

### Challenges
- Requires consistent communication  
- Unclear user stories can cause confusion  
- Frequent changes must be carefully managed  

## Conclusion
The Scrum Agile framework supported the successful development of the SNHU Travel booking software by promoting collaboration, adaptability, and continuous improvement. Working in multiple roles provided a deeper understanding of the development process and reinforced the importance of communication and teamwork.

Overall, this experience demonstrates that Agile practices can lead to improved team performance and higher quality software products.
